Summit the Highest Trekking Peak in Ladakh at 6,153m

6,153m • Best Season: July-September

About the Expedition

The Stok Kangri Expedition is the ultimate challenge for trekkers looking to summit a Himalayan peak without technical climbing equipment. Standing at 6,153 meters (20,187 feet), Stok Kangri is the highest trekking peak in India's Ladakh region. This expedition tests your endurance, acclimatization, and mental strength as you navigate glaciers, high passes, and the final steep ascent to the summit. The reward? Breathtaking 360° views of the Zanskar Range, Karakoram peaks, and even distant views of K2 on clear days. Our guided expedition includes comprehensive acclimatization, expert guides, and all necessary support to maximize your chances of a successful summit.

Expedition Itinerary

Arrival in Leh (3,500m)

Arrive at Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport in Leh. Rest day for acclimatization. Briefing about the expedition in the evening. Overnight at hotel.

Light walking tour of Leh including visits to Shanti Stupa and Leh Palace. Medical check-up and gear inspection. Final preparations for the trek.

Leh to Stok Village (3,600m) to Chang Ma (4,300m)

Drive to Stok village (1hr). Begin trek following the Stok Kangri trail along the Stok river. Gradual ascent to Chang Ma campsite.

Rest day with short acclimatization hike to 5,200m. Final gear check, ice axe/crampon practice. Early dinner and rest for summit attempt.

Start at 12am. Ascend glacier using crampons, reach summit by sunrise. Descend carefully to base camp. Celebration dinner.

The Challenge

High Altitude

At 6,153m, the thin air contains only about 50% of the oxygen at sea level. Proper acclimatization is crucial to avoid altitude sickness.

Extreme Cold

Summit temperatures can drop to -20°C with wind chill. Proper layering and cold weather gear are essential for safety.

Glacier Crossing

The final ascent involves crossing a glacier requiring crampons and ice axes. Previous experience with this equipment is highly recommended.

Long Summit Day

The 12-14 hour summit push starts at midnight, testing your endurance with 1,200m of ascent and descent in one day.

Weather Uncertainty

High winds, snow, or whiteout conditions can turn back even well-prepared teams. We build in buffer days for multiple attempts.

Physical Demands

Requires excellent cardiovascular fitness and leg strength. Participants should be comfortable carrying 8-10kg packs for long durations.

Preparation Guide

Fitness Preparation

  • 3-6 months of targeted training
  • Build to 5-6 hour hikes with 15kg pack
  • Focus on endurance and leg strength
  • Include stair climbing with weight
  • Altitude simulation if possible

Essential Gear

  • -20°C sleeping bag
  • Mountaineering boots (B2/B3)
  • Crampons & ice axe
  • 4-season tent (provided)
  • High-altitude clothing system

Acclimatization

  • Arrive in Leh 2 days early
  • Stay hydrated (4-5L/day)
  • Diamox can help (consult doctor)
  • Listen to your body
  • Gradual ascent profile
