Puri, Odisha

Sacred Beaches, Ancient Temples & The Legendary Rath Yatra

28°C • Humid

Overview

Puri, one of India's four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites, is a vibrant coastal city in Odisha renowned for the 12th-century Jagannath Temple and its annual Rath Yatra festival. This spiritual hub combines golden beaches with rich cultural heritage, where the rhythmic waves of the Bay of Bengal meet ancient temple chants. Puri's unique beach allows devotees to pay respects to their ancestors, while the temple's mysterious rituals and the unfinished top of its main tower continue to intrigue visitors. The city's vibrant handicrafts, especially intricate Pattachitra paintings and stone carvings, reflect its artistic legacy.

Jagannath Temple

The iconic 12th-century temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath (Krishna), his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra. Famous for its annual Rath Yatra where the deities are taken out in massive chariots. The temple's kitchen feeds thousands daily with Mahaprasad.

Free Entry
Beach
Golden Sands

Puri Beach

One of India's most sacred beaches where pilgrims perform rituals for ancestors. The beach offers stunning sunrises, camel rides, and fresh seafood. The unique phenomenon of no waves crashing at the Jagannath Temple's sea-facing side adds to its spiritual significance.

4.7 (12,730 reviews)
Free
UNESCO
Architectural Marvel

Konark Sun Temple

The magnificent 13th-century Sun Temple shaped like a giant chariot with 24 wheels, pulled by 7 horses. Located 35km from Puri, this UNESCO site features intricate stone carvings depicting various aspects of life. The temple's alignment with the sun is an engineering marvel.

4.8 (10,520 reviews)
Entry: ₹40
Festival
Grand Procession

Rath Yatra

The world-famous annual chariot festival (June/July) where the deities are taken from Jagannath Temple to Gundicha Temple in massive, elaborately decorated chariots. Millions gather to pull the ropes of the chariots in this spectacular display of devotion.

5.0 (18,980 reviews)
Free
Market
Handicrafts

Pipili Village

The colorful appliqué work village near Puri known for its vibrant canopies, umbrellas, and bags. Watch artisans create these beautiful pieces and shop for souvenirs. The village supplies all fabric decorations for the Jagannath Temple and Rath Yatra.

4.5 (6,250 reviews)
Shopping: ₹200-5000
Wildlife
Lagoon

Chilika Lake

Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, about 50km from Puri. A biodiversity hotspot hosting Irrawaddy dolphins, migratory birds (winter), and numerous fish species. Take a boat cruise to spot dolphins and visit the scenic Kalijai Temple on an island.

4.6 (8,820 reviews)
Cruise: ₹300-800

Best Time to Visit

Winter (Oct-Feb)

18°C - 28°C
Peak Season
Pleasant weather perfect for temple visits and beach activities. Ideal for sightseeing with clear skies. Peak pilgrimage season with many festivals.

Spring (Mar-May)

25°C - 35°C
Shoulder Season
Hotter days but less crowded. Good for beach lovers who don't mind the heat. Holi and other spring festivals bring cultural vibrancy.

Monsoon (Jun-Sep)

28°C - 32°C
Rath Yatra Season
Humid with heavy rains, but the spectacular Rath Yatra occurs in June/July. Beaches may have strong currents. Lush green landscapes.
