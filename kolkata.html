Kolkata, West Bengal

The City of Joy - Where Culture, History and Modernity Collide

32°C • Humid

Overview

Kolkata, formerly Calcutta, is the cultural capital of India and the capital of West Bengal. This vibrant city on the banks of the Hooghly River is known for its colonial architecture, intellectual heritage, artistic soul, and of course, its legendary love for food. From the iconic Howrah Bridge to the bustling markets of New Market, from the intellectual cafes of College Street to the spiritual aura of Dakshineswar Kali Temple, Kolkata offers a sensory overload that stays with you forever.

Victoria Memorial

Majestic white marble museum built in memory of Queen Victoria, surrounded by lush gardens. Houses an impressive collection of colonial-era artifacts, paintings, and manuscripts. The light and sound show in the evenings is spectacular.

Howrah Bridge

The iconic cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River, one of the busiest bridges in the world. Best experienced at dawn when the city wakes up or at sunset when the lights come on. The nearby flower market is a photographer's delight.

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Famous Hindu temple on the banks of Hooghly River where Ramakrishna Paramahamsa served as priest. The temple complex with its 12 identical Shiva temples offers a serene spiritual experience away from the city chaos.

Indian Museum

Established in 1814, this is the oldest and largest museum in India. Houses rare collections of antiques, armor, ornaments, fossils, skeletons, and Mughal paintings. The Egyptian mummy is a star attraction.

College Street (Boi Para)

The largest second-hand book market in the world, with kilometer after kilometer of book stalls. Home to historic coffee houses where intellectuals like Tagore and Satyajit Ray once debated. A paradise for bibliophiles.

Kolkata's Legendary Cuisine

Rosogolla

Kolkata's famous spongy cottage cheese balls soaked in sugar syrup. The city's signature sweet that started the rosogolla wars with Odisha. Best enjoyed fresh from KC Das or Balaram Mullick.

Best at: KC Das, Esplanade

Kathi Roll

Kolkata's gift to street food - paratha wrapped around kebabs, eggs and spices. Originally from Nizam's, now available across the city. Perfect on-the-go meal that packs a flavor punch.

Best at: Nizam's, New Market

Macher Jhol

Traditional Bengali fish curry with potatoes in a light gravy. The comfort food of Kolkata, best enjoyed with steaming rice. Each household has its own secret recipe passed down generations.

Best at: 6 Ballygunge Place

Kolkata Chow Mein

Unique Indian-Chinese fusion noodles with green chilies, soy sauce and local spices. Tangra's Chinatown offers the most authentic versions of this Kolkata staple.

Best at: Tung Fong, Tangra

Puchka

Kolkata's version of pani puri - crisp hollow puris filled with potato mash and dunked in tangy tamarind water. Best enjoyed at roadside stalls where the vendor customizes the spice level.

Best at: Vivekananda Park stalls

Mishti Doi

Sweetened yogurt set in earthen pots that impart a unique earthy flavor. The caramelized top layer is the best part. A must-have dessert after any Bengali meal.

Best at: Ganguram, multiple locations

Best Time to Visit

Winter

12°C - 25°C
Oct - Feb
Pleasant weather ideal for sightseeing. Durga Puja (Oct) and Christmas/New Year celebrations make this peak tourist season. Book accommodations well in advance.

Summer

28°C - 38°C
Mar - Jun
Hot and humid weather. Early mornings and evenings best for outdoor activities. Good hotel deals available. Indoor attractions comfortable to visit.

Monsoon

25°C - 32°C
Jul - Sep
Heavy rainfall can disrupt travel plans but the city looks beautiful in the rain. Fewer crowds and lush green surroundings. Carry rain gear.
