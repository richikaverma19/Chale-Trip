About Bandhavgarh

Bandhavgarh National Park is one of India's most famous tiger reserves, located in the Umaria district of Madhya Pradesh. Known for its high density of Royal Bengal Tigers, the park covers an area of 1,536 sq km with a core area of 716 sq km.

The park gets its name from the ancient Bandhavgarh Fort that sits atop a hill within the reserve. With a rich biodiversity that includes leopards, deer, and over 250 species of birds, Bandhavgarh offers one of the best wildlife experiences in India.

Safari Zones

Bandhavgarh is divided into several safari zones to manage tourism effectively:

  • Tala Zone - Most popular with highest tiger sightings
  • Magadhi Zone - Excellent for wildlife photography
  • Khitauli Zone - Known for diverse flora and fauna
  • Panpatha Zone - Newest addition to the park

Safaris are conducted twice daily - in the morning and afternoon. Advance booking is recommended, especially during peak season (October-June).

Wildlife at Bandhavgarh

Mammals

Bandhavgarh is home to:

  • Royal Bengal Tiger
  • Leopard
  • Spotted Deer (Chital)
  • Sambar Deer
  • Wild Boar
  • Indian Bison (Gaur)

Birds

Over 250 species including:

  • Malabar Pied Hornbill
  • Indian Pitta
  • White-bellied Drongo
  • Various species of eagles and owls
