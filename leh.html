Trekking in Ladakh

Ladakh offers some of the most spectacular high-altitude treks in the world, with breathtaking landscapes that range from rugged mountains to serene lakes and ancient monasteries. Trekking here is a unique experience that combines physical challenge with spiritual enrichment, as you traverse through remote villages and Buddhist cultural sites. The region's stark beauty, clear blue skies, and dramatic landscapes make every step worthwhile. From the famous Markha Valley trek to the challenging Chadar trek on frozen rivers, Ladakh has adventures for all levels of trekkers.

Trek Difficulty Levels

Easy Treks

  • 4-6 hours walking per day
  • Max altitude: 4,000m
  • Gentle ascents/descents
  • Good paths and trails
  • Example: Sham Valley Trek

Moderate Treks

  • 5-7 hours walking per day
  • Max altitude: 4,800m
  • Some steep sections
  • Basic acclimatization needed
  • Example: Markha Valley Trek

Challenging Treks

  • 7-9 hours walking per day
  • Max altitude: 5,600m+
  • High passes and steep terrain
  • Requires good fitness
  • Example: Stok Kangri Expedition

Popular Ladakh Treks

Markha Valley Trek

75 km 7-8 days
  • Highest point: Kongmaru La (5,260m)
  • Stay in village homestays
  • Visit ancient monasteries
  • Cross three high passes

Stok Kangri Trek

40 km 8-9 days
  • Summit at 6,153m
  • Glacier crossing required
  • High altitude camping
  • Requires technical skills

Sham Valley Trek

50 km 4-5 days
  • Max altitude: 3,900m
  • Authentic Ladakhi cuisine
  • Village stays with locals
  • Visit Lamayuru Monastery

Chadar Trek

105 km 9-10 days
  • Walk on frozen Zanskar River
  • Temperatures: -15°C to -30°C
  • Stay in caves along the river
  • Only in January-February

Rupshu Valley Trek

90 km 8-9 days
  • Remote nomadic region
  • Visit Tso Moriri lake
  • Meet Changpa nomads
  • Cross Parang La pass (5,600m)

Nubra Valley Trek

60 km 6-7 days
  • Cross Khardung La (5,359m)
  • See rare double-humped camels
  • Visit Diskit Monastery
  • Views of Siachen Glacier

Best Time for Trekking

May-June

5°C - 20°C
Early Season
Snow melting, rivers full. Fewer trekkers. Some high passes may still be snowbound. Ideal for lower altitude treks.

July-August

10°C - 25°C
Peak Season
Warmest months with stable weather. All passes accessible. More crowded. Occasional afternoon showers.

September

0°C - 15°C
Late Season
Cooler temperatures, clear skies. Fewer trekkers. Best for photography. Some services start closing.

January-February

-15°C - -30°C
Chadar Trek
Only time for the frozen river trek. Extreme cold but unique experience. Requires special preparation.
