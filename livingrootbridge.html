Living Root Bridges Trek, Meghalaya

Discover Nature's Architectural Marvels in the Lush Khasi Hills

22°C • Rainforest

Overview

The Living Root Bridges of Meghalaya are a unique natural phenomenon created by the Khasi tribes by training the roots of rubber trees (Ficus elastica) to grow across rivers, forming sturdy bridges that strengthen over time. These incredible living structures, some over 100 years old, are found in the dense rainforests of the East Khasi Hills. The most famous is the double-decker root bridge in Nongriat village, accessible via a challenging trek through breathtaking landscapes with waterfalls and natural pools. This trek offers a glimpse into the sustainable engineering practices of the Khasi people and the stunning biodiversity of Meghalaya's rainforests.

Best Time to Visit

Winter (Nov-Feb)

10°C - 20°C
Peak Season
Pleasant weather with cool temperatures ideal for trekking. Clear skies and minimal rain. Best visibility for photography and most comfortable for the strenuous hike.

Spring (Mar-May)

15°C - 25°C
Shoulder Season
Warmer but still good for visits. The forest comes alive with blooming flowers. Occasional showers make the waterfalls more spectacular.

Monsoon (Jun-Sep)

18°C - 28°C
Off Season
Heavy rains make trails slippery and challenging. Leeches are common. But the rainforest is at its lushest and waterfalls most powerful. Only for adventurous trekkers.
