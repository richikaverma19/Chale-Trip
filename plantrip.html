General Travel Safety Tips

  • Make digital and physical copies of important documents (passport, ID, travel insurance)
  • Share your itinerary with someone at home and check in regularly
  • Research local emergency numbers and the location of your country's embassy
  • Be aware of local customs and dress codes to avoid unwanted attention
  • Keep valuables in your hotel safe and only carry what you need for the day
  • Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, remove yourself from the situation

Health Precautions

  • Check if any vaccinations are required or recommended for your destination
  • Pack a basic first aid kit with bandages, antiseptic, pain relievers, and any personal medications
  • Know the location of nearby hospitals or clinics at your destination
  • Be cautious with street food - look for vendors with high turnover and cooked-to-order items
  • Drink bottled water in areas where tap water isn't safe
  • Protect yourself from sun exposure with sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses

Transportation Safety

  • Only use licensed taxis or reputable ride-sharing services
  • Research public transportation routes and schedules in advance
  • Be cautious when driving in foreign countries - understand local traffic laws first
  • Keep your belongings secure and in sight at all times on buses and trains
  • At airports, never leave luggage unattended or accept packages from strangers
  • Consider travel insurance that covers rental car damages

Avoiding Common Scams

  • Beware of overly friendly strangers offering unsolicited help or tours
  • Avoid gambling games or "free" gifts in tourist areas
  • Only exchange money at authorized currency exchanges or banks
  • Watch for taxi drivers who "forget" to turn on the meter
  • Be skeptical of "closed" attractions with someone offering alternative tours
  • Research common scams specific to your destination before you go

Money Safety Tips

  • Notify your bank of your travel plans to avoid card freezes
  • Carry a mix of payment methods (cash, credit cards, debit cards)
  • Keep money in multiple places in case of theft
  • Use ATMs inside banks during business hours when possible
  • Know the exchange rate to avoid being short-changed
  • Consider a money belt or hidden pouch for valuables
