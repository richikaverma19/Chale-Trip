Markha Valley Trek

Ladakh's Classic Himalayan Trek Through Buddhist Villages & High Passes

5°C - 25°C • Best June-September

Trek Overview

The Markha Valley Trek is Ladakh's most famous and diverse trek, offering a perfect blend of breathtaking landscapes, Buddhist culture, and adventurous high passes. This 7-8 day trek takes you through the heart of Hemis National Park, crossing three high passes over 5,000m, visiting remote monasteries, and staying in traditional Ladakhi villages. The trail follows the Markha River through spectacular canyons, past ancient mani walls, and offers stunning views of the Zanskar and Stok mountain ranges. Rated as moderate difficulty, it's accessible to fit beginners while still offering challenges for experienced trekkers.

Essential Information

Fitness Preparation

This trek requires good cardiovascular fitness. Prepare with 2-3 months of regular hiking, stair climbing, and endurance training. Altitude adds significant challenge, so arrive in Leh 2-3 days early to acclimatize.

Packing Essentials

Must-haves: sturdy trekking boots, -10°C sleeping bag, layered clothing, sun protection, headlamp, water purification, basic first aid kit. Porters carry 10kg max, so pack light but adequately for variable mountain weather.

Altitude Considerations

Proper acclimatization is crucial. Watch for headache, nausea, dizziness. Descend if symptoms worsen. Drink 4-5L water daily, avoid alcohol, eat carbs, and consider Diamox after consulting your doctor.

Permits & Regulations

Requires Inner Line Permit for foreigners (arranged in Leh). Indian nationals need ID proof. Camping in designated areas only. No plastic, carry all trash out. Respect local customs and monasteries.

Food & Water

Homestays serve local Ladakhi meals (tsampa, thukpa, momos). Carry snacks like nuts, chocolate, energy bars. Treat all water with tablets/filters. Hot drinks provided at camps.

Cost & Budget

Budget ₹25,000-40,000 for guided trek including permits, food, accommodation, transport. Additional costs: gear rental, tips (₹300-500/day for team), personal expenses. ATMs only in Leh.

Best Time to Trek

June

5°C - 18°C
Early Season
Fewer trekkers, snow melting creating full rivers. Some high passes may still have snow. Wildflowers begin blooming. Cooler temperatures.

July-August

10°C - 25°C
Peak Season
Warmest months with most stable weather. All passes accessible. More crowded. Lush green landscapes. Occasional afternoon showers possible.

September

0°C - 15°C
Late Season
Cooler temperatures, crystal clear skies. Fewer trekkers. Stunning autumn colors. Some services start winding down. Chance of early snow.
