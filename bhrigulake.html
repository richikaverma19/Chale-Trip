Bhrigu Lake Trek, Himachal Pradesh

High-Altitude Alpine Lake with Panoramic Himalayan Views

5°C • Chilly

Overview

The Bhrigu Lake Trek is a mesmerizing high-altitude Himalayan trek in the Kullu Valley of Himachal Pradesh. Named after Maharishi Bhrigu, this sacred alpine lake sits at an elevation of 4,300 meters (14,100 ft) surrounded by lush meadows and snow-capped peaks. The trek offers breathtaking views of the Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar ranges, passing through beautiful landscapes of forests, grasslands, and glacial moraines. Considered moderately difficult, this 4-day trek is perfect for both beginners and experienced trekkers looking for a short but rewarding Himalayan adventure.

How to Reach Bhrigu Lake

10 hrs from airport

  • Taxi fare to Gulaba: ₹1500-2000
  • Helicopter services available from Kullu
12-14 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Nearest major station: Joginder Nagar - 150km away
  • Alternative: Chandigarh - 280km away
  • Recommended trains: Himalayan Queen
  • Taxi/bus options available from station
  • Scenic mountain route from Mandi
10-12 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH3 and NH154
  • Delhi-Manali via Mandi, Kullu (550km)
  • HRTC buses (₹800-1200 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹6000-8000 one way)
  • Stunning views of Beas River valley

Trekking Routes to Bhrigu Lake

Standard Route (Gulaba)

14 km 4 days
  • Route: Gulaba → Rola Kholi → Bhrigu Lake → Gulaba
  • Stay at: Campsites with basic facilities
  • Views: Seven Sisters Peaks, Hanuman Tibba
  • Max Altitude: 4,300m at lake

Vashisht Route

18 km 5 days
  • Route: Vashisht → Pandu Ropa → Bhrigu Lake → Gulaba
  • Stay at: Village homestays and camps
  • Visit: Vashisht Hot Springs before trek
  • Scenic forest trails initially

Extended Trek

25 km 6 days
  • Route: Gulaba → Bhrigu Lake → Pandu Ropa → Vashisht
  • Includes: Both standard routes combined
  • Explore: More meadows and viewpoints
  • More time for acclimatization

Trek Highlights

Alpine Lake

Visit the sacred Bhrigu Lake at 4,300m that never completely freezes despite sub-zero temperatures

Panoramic Views

360° views of Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar ranges including Seven Sisters peaks and Hanuman Tibba

Meadow Camping

Camp in beautiful high-altitude meadows under starry skies at Rola Kholi (3,650m)

Snow Walks

Walk on snow patches (depending on season) during the final ascent to the lake

Diverse Landscapes

Experience changing landscapes from forests to grasslands to moraines

Hot Springs

Relax in Vashisht's natural hot springs after completing your trek

Best Time to Visit

Summer (May-Jun)

10°C - 20°C
Pre-Monsoon
Pleasant temperatures with clear skies. Meadows turn green with wildflowers. Some snow patches remain near lake.

Monsoon (Jul-Aug)

8°C - 18°C
Lush Greenery
Landscape becomes lush but rain can make trails slippery. Leeches possible in forest sections.

Autumn (Sep-Oct)

5°C - 15°C
Post-Monsoon
Stable weather with crisp air. Stunning autumn colors in lower regions. Best for clear mountain views.
