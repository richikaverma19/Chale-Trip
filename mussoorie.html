Mussoorie, Uttarakhand

Queen of the Hills - Where the Clouds Dance on Mountain Peaks

18°C • Pleasant

Overview

Perched at an altitude of 6,170 feet in the Garhwal Himalayas, Mussoorie is one of India's most famous hill stations. Known as the "Queen of the Hills," it offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks and the Doon Valley. Established in 1823 as a British hill station, Mussoorie retains its colonial charm with Gothic architecture, winding roads, and lush green hills. The Mall Road buzzes with activity, while Camel's Back Road offers serene sunset views. From adventure activities like trekking and paragliding to leisurely walks through cloud-kissed hills, Mussoorie provides the perfect mountain getaway.

How to Reach Mussoorie

1 hr from Dehradun

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Jolly Grant (DED) - 60km away
  • Direct flights from Delhi (1hr flight)
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, SpiceJet
  • Taxis available (₹1500-2000 to Mussoorie)
  • Alternative airport: Delhi (290km away)
6-7 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Nearest major station: Dehradun (35km away)
  • Shatabdi Express (5hrs from Delhi to Dehradun)
  • Other options: Nanda Devi Express, Mussoorie Express
  • Shared taxis available from Dehradun station (₹100-150 per person)
  • Pre-paid taxi stand at station (₹800-1200 to Mussoorie)
7-8 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH7 and Mussoorie Road
  • Delhi-Mussoorie via Meerut, Muzaffarnagar, Dehradun
  • Volvo/AC buses from Delhi (₹700-1200 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹4000-6000 one way)
  • Major bus operators: Uttarakhand Transport, RedBus

Gun Hill

The second highest point in Mussoorie offering panoramic views of the Himalayas. Reachable by cable car or a scenic 1.5km trek. The sunset views are spectacular, and the area has charming cafes and souvenir shops.

Popular Activities in Mussoorie

Sunset at Lal Tibba

Witness breathtaking sunset views over the Himalayan peaks from the highest point in Mussoorie. Use the vintage telescope to get closer views of snow-capped mountains including Bandarpoonch and Srikanth peaks.

Lal Tibba

Trek to Nag Tibba

Popular weekend trek to the "Serpent's Peak" offering 360° views of Himalayan ranges. The 15km round trip passes through oak forests and meadows. Camp overnight for spectacular sunrise views (guides available).

Nag Tibba Base

Colonial Heritage Walk

Explore Mussoorie's British-era architecture including Christ Church, Savoy Hotel, and Wynberg-Allen School. Learn about the town's history as a summer capital during the Raj. Ends with high tea at a heritage property.

Mall Road Area

Paragliding at Bhatta Falls

Tandem paragliding flights over the Doon Valley with professional pilots. Soar like a bird with panoramic views of Mussoorie hills. Includes training session and safety equipment. Best done in morning hours.

Bhatta Falls

Cable Car to Gun Hill

Scenic 5-minute cable car ride offering spectacular aerial views of Mussoorie and Doon Valley. At the top, enjoy local snacks while taking in panoramic mountain views. Souvenir shops and photo spots available.

Gun Hill Station

Tibetan Market Shopping

Explore the vibrant Tibetan Market near Library Chowk for woolens, handicrafts, and souvenirs. Bargain for woolen shawls, Tibetan singing bowls, and silver jewelry. Don't miss trying thukpa and momos at local stalls.

Tibetan Market

Best Time to Visit

Spring

15°C - 25°C
Mar - Apr
Pleasant weather with blooming rhododendrons. Ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Summer crowds begin to arrive by April.

Summer

18°C - 30°C
May - Jun
Peak season with cool mountain air escaping plains' heat. Expect crowds but perfect for family vacations. Book accommodations in advance.

Monsoon

15°C - 22°C
Jul - Aug
Lush green landscapes with frequent rains. Fewer tourists but some activities may be limited. Risk of landslides on approach roads.

Autumn/Winter

5°C - 18°C
Sep - Feb
Clear skies with spectacular mountain views. December-January can be chilly with possible snowfall at higher altitudes.
