Hampi, Karnataka

The Ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire - UNESCO World Heritage Site

32°C • Sunny

Overview

Hampi, the legendary capital of the Vijayanagara Empire (14th-16th century), is an open-air museum of history, architecture, and culture spread across 26 square kilometers. Located on the banks of the Tungabhadra River amidst a surreal boulder-strewn landscape, Hampi's magnificent ruins tell tales of a prosperous kingdom that once rivaled Rome and Beijing in grandeur.

How to Reach Hampi

4 hrs from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Vidyanagar (Bellary) - 40km away
  • Alternative: Hubli airport - 160km away
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, SpiceJet
  • Taxi fare to Hampi: ₹1500-2000
  • Best connection: Bangalore airport
8-10 hrs from Bangalore

By Train

  • Nearest railhead: Hospet Junction (13km)
  • Direct trains from Bangalore, Hyderabad
  • Recommended trains: Hampi Express
  • Auto fare to Hampi: ₹300-400
  • Bus options available from station
6-7 hrs from Bangalore

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH50 and SH131
  • Bangalore-Hampi via Chitradurga, Hospet
  • KSRTC buses (₹500-800 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹4000-6000 one way)
  • Scenic route through rural Karnataka

Hampi's Local Cuisine

Bisi Bele Bath

Traditional Karnataka rice dish cooked with lentils, vegetables and special spice mix. Served hot with ghee and papad.

Best at: Mango Tree Restaurant

Masala Dosa

Crispy rice crepe stuffed with spiced potato filling, served with coconut chutney and sambar. Breakfast staple.

Best at: Gouthami Guest House

Kundapur Chicken

Spicy coastal Karnataka chicken curry with coconut and kundapur masala. Best enjoyed with neer dosa or rice.

Best at: Ravi's Rose Restaurant

Holige

Sweet flatbread stuffed with jaggery and lentil/chana dal paste. Hampi's version of puran poli.

Best at: Local Sweet Shops

Mirchi Bajji

Deep-fried chili fritters made with large green chilies dipped in chickpea batter. Perfect monsoon snack.

Best at: Hampi Bazaar Stalls

Maddur Vada

Crispy fried snack made from semolina, rice flour and spices. Originating from nearby Maddur town.

Best at: Kamat Hotel, Hospet
