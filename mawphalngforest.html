Mawphlang Sacred Forest

Step into a mystical world where nature and spirituality intertwine in Meghalaya's most revered forest

About Mawphlang Sacred Forest

The Mawphlang Sacred Forest is one of Meghalaya's most famous and well-preserved sacred groves, covering about 78 hectares. This ancient forest, protected by the Khasi people for centuries, is a living example of indigenous conservation practices where nature and spirituality are deeply interconnected.

According to Khasi beliefs, the forest is protected by the local deity Labasa, who punishes those who harm the forest or take anything from it. Even a leaf picked from the forest is said to bring misfortune unless taken with permission through proper rituals.

Forest Walk Experience

Your visit to Mawphlang Sacred Forest includes:

  • Guided tour with a local Khasi guide who shares forest legends and traditions
  • 1.5 km nature trail through dense, untouched vegetation
  • Viewing of sacrificial altars (mawkynthei) where rituals are performed
  • Learning about medicinal plants used by the Khasi people
  • Seeing ancient monoliths and understanding their significance
  • Opportunity to witness a traditional ritual (on request, additional cost)

Ecological Significance

The forest serves as an important biodiversity reservoir with:

  • Over 200 plant species, including many medicinal plants
  • Rare orchids and ferns found only in these sacred groves
  • Rich birdlife including the Khasi Hills' endemic species
  • Ancient oak and rhododendron trees hundreds of years old
  • A unique mycorrhizal network connecting trees underground

Cultural Importance

The forest is central to Khasi cultural identity:

  • Site of annual religious ceremonies by local villages
  • Traditional healing practices still conducted using forest plants
  • Stories and oral traditions passed down through generations
  • Living example of indigenous conservation ethics
  • Connection to the Khasi matrilineal society's worldview

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit: Year-round, but most lush during monsoon (June-September)

Duration: 1.5-2 hours for standard guided walk

Difficulty: Easy (well-maintained paths with some uneven terrain)

What to Bring: Comfortable walking shoes, rain gear (in monsoon), camera, water

Photography: Permitted but commercial photography requires permission
