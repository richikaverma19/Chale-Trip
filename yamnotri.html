Yamunotri, Uttarakhand

The Sacred Source of the Yamuna River - First Stop of the Char Dham Yatra

12°C • Chilly

Overview

Yamunotri, nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas at an altitude of 3,293 meters, is the sacred source of the Yamuna River and the first stop in the revered Char Dham Yatra. The main attraction is the Yamunotri Temple dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, surrounded by breathtaking mountain scenery and hot water springs. The spiritual journey to Yamunotri through rugged Himalayan terrain is as rewarding as the destination itself.

Trekking Routes to Yamunotri

Janki Chatti to Yamunotri

6 km 3-4 hours
  • Standard trek route for pilgrims
  • Tea shops along the way
  • Views of Bandarpoonch peak
  • Pony/Palki available (₹1500-3000)

Hanuman Chatti to Yamunotri

13 km 6-7 hours
  • Longer but scenic route
  • Basic dhabas available
  • Passes through dense forests
  • Steeper ascent than Janki Chatti route

Kharsali to Yamunotri

8 km 4-5 hours
  • Less crowded alternative route
  • Village homestays available
  • Views of Yamuna valley
  • Kharsali has winter deity residence

Must-Visit Attractions

Main Temple
Top Rated
Sacred Temple
Built in 1839 by Tehri Naresh

Yamunotri Temple

The sacred shrine dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, built near the thermal springs. The black marble idol of Yamuna is worshipped here. The temple remains closed during winter when the deity is brought to Kharsali village.

4.9 (8,210 reviews)
Free Entry
Sacred Site
Natural Wonder
Hot Spring

Surya Kund

Boiling hot spring near the temple where pilgrims cook rice and potatoes as prasad by tying them in cloth bags and dipping in the hot water. The water temperature remains near boiling point throughout the year.

4.7 (5,430 reviews)
Free Entry
Trekking
Sacred Rock

Divya Shila

A sacred rock pillar near Surya Kund that pilgrims worship before entering the Yamunotri Temple. It's believed to be the divine stone that marks the origin of the Yamuna River.

4.5 (3,120 reviews)
Free Entry
Viewpoint
River Source

Yamuna Glacier (Actual Source)

The actual source of Yamuna River at Champasar Glacier (4,421m), a 1km steep trek from the temple. Only experienced trekkers attempt this challenging route to witness the glacier-fed lake.

Yamunotri's Local Cuisine

Surya Kund Prasad

Rice and potatoes cooked in the hot springs of Surya Kund as offering to Yamuna. Devotees carry this simple yet sacred prasad back home.

Available at: Surya Kund near temple

Aloo Ke Gutke

Traditional Garhwali dish of boiled potatoes tempered with local spices like jakhiya and bhangjeera. Served with puris or rotis.

Best at: GMVN Tourist Rest House

Bhang Ki Chutney

Spicy chutney made from hemp seeds, a local delicacy in Garhwal region. Served with meals or as accompaniment.

Best at: Local dhabas enroute

Buransh Tea

Herbal tea made from rhododendron flowers, a local specialty that helps with altitude acclimatization.

Best at: Trek route tea stalls

Kaafuli

Traditional Garhwali thick gravy made from spinach-like leafy greens, served with steamed rice or roti.

Best at: Barkot local restaurants

Mandua Ki Roti

Nutritious flatbread made from finger millet flour, staple diet of Garhwal region. Served with ghee and local dal.

Best at: Kharsali homestays

Best Time to Visit

Summer Season

10°C - 20°C
May - June
Pleasant weather ideal for trekking. Temple opens on Akshaya Tritiya (usually May). Clear mountain views and comfortable temperatures.

Monsoon Season

15°C - 25°C
July - Aug
Lush green landscapes but trekking can be slippery. Occasional landslides may disrupt travel. Fewer crowds.

Autumn Season

5°C - 15°C
Sep - Nov
Best time with clear skies and comfortable temperatures. Temple closes on Diwali (Oct-Nov). Peak season for pilgrims.
