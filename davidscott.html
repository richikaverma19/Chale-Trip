David Scott Trail, Meghalaya

Historic 19th Century Trek Through Khasi Hills & Living Root Bridges

18°C • Misty

Overview

The David Scott Trail is a 16km historic trek in Meghalaya that follows a 200-year-old horse cart route built by British officer David Scott. This scenic trail connects Mawphlang to Lad Mawphlang, passing through lush forests, waterfalls, traditional Khasi villages, and offering breathtaking views of the Umiam Valley. Rated as moderately difficult, the trek takes 5-7 hours to complete and showcases Meghalaya's unique living root bridges, sacred groves, and vibrant local culture. The trail is named after David Scott, a British administrator who served in Northeast India during the early 19th century.

How to Reach David Scott Trail

1 hr from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Shillong (Umroi) - 35km away
  • Alternative: Guwahati (Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi) - 120km away
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, Air India
  • Taxi fare to Mawphlang: ₹1500-2000
  • Helicopter services available from Guwahati
3-4 hrs from Guwahati

By Train

  • Nearest major station: Guwahati - 120km away
  • Recommended trains: Rajdhani Express, Brahmaputra Mail
  • Shared taxis available from station to Shillong
  • Local buses to Mawphlang from Shillong
  • Scenic mountain route through Umiam Lake
2-3 hrs from Shillong

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH6 and state highways
  • Shillong to Mawphlang: 25km (1 hr)
  • MST buses (₹50-100 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹1000-1500 one way)
  • Stunning views of Khasi Hills en route

Living Root Bridges

Several living root bridges can be seen along extended routes. These remarkable bio-engineering feats are grown from rubber tree roots by Khasi tribes over 15-30 years.
