About Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park, located in Madhya Pradesh, is one of India's largest and most scenic wildlife reserves. Spread over 940 sq km, it inspired Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book." The park is famous for its significant population of Royal Bengal Tigers, leopards, sloth bears, barasingha (swamp deer), and diverse bird species.

Established in 1955 and declared a Tiger Reserve in 1973 under Project Tiger, Kanha is renowned for its successful conservation programs, particularly for the hard-ground barasingha.

Wildlife at Kanha

  • Mammals: Bengal Tiger, Leopard, Sloth Bear, Indian Wild Dog (Dhole), Barasingha, Spotted Deer, Sambar, Gaur (Indian Bison)
  • Birds: Over 300 species including the Indian Pitta, Crested Serpent Eagle, Paradise Flycatcher, and various owls
  • Reptiles: Python, Indian Cobra, Monitor Lizard

Safari Information

Safari Zones:

Kanha is divided into four main zones: Kanha, Kisli, Mukki, and Sarhi. Each zone offers unique landscapes and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Safari Timings:

Morning Safari: 6:00 AM to 11:00 AM (varies seasonally)
Evening Safari: 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM (varies seasonally)

Booking:

Safaris can be booked online through the official Madhya Pradesh Forest Department website or through authorized tour operators.
