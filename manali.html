Manali, Himachal Pradesh

Queen of the Hills - Gateway to the Himalayas

12°C • Snowing

Overview

Nestled in the breathtaking Beas River Valley, Manali is a picturesque hill station surrounded by snow-capped peaks, lush forests, and gushing rivers. This Himalayan paradise offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, adventure sports, and rich cultural heritage. From thrilling activities like paragliding and skiing to serene walks through apple orchards and visits to ancient temples, Manali has something for every traveler.

4.7/5 (2.3K reviews)
Best Season: Oct-Jun
550km from Delhi
Ideal Duration: 4-7 days

How to Reach Manali

2 hrs from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Bhuntar (Kullu) - 50km away
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Chandigarh
  • Major airlines: Air India, SpiceJet
  • Taxi fare to Manali: ₹1500-2000
  • Alternative: Chandigarh airport (300km away)
12-14 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Nearest railhead: Joginder Nagar (165km)
  • Best option: Ambala Cantt or Chandigarh
  • Recommended trains: Himalayan Queen, Kalka Shatabdi
  • Overnight Volvo buses available from station
  • Taxi from Chandigarh: ₹4000-5000
12-14 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH1 and NH3
  • Delhi-Manali via Chandigarh, Bilaspur, Mandi
  • HRTC Volvo buses (₹1000-1500 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹6000-8000 one way)
  • Scenic but challenging mountain roads

Scenic Road Trip Routes to Manali

Delhi to Manali

550 km 12-14 hours
  • Route: NH1 to Chandigarh, then NH21
  • Stop at: Sukhna Lake, Chandigarh
  • Photo-op: Pandoh Dam, Mandi
  • Major fuel stations along highway

Chandigarh to Manali

300 km 7-8 hours
  • Route: NH21 via Bilaspur, Mandi
  • Stop at: Rewalsar Lake
  • Visit: Great Himalayan National Park
  • Optional: Kullu Valley detour

Shimla to Manali

250 km 8-9 hours
  • Route: NH205 to Narkanda, then NH505
  • Stop at: Jalori Pass (10,800 ft)
  • Photo-op: Serolsar Lake
  • Break journey in Banjar valley

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

Rohtang Pass

Breathtaking high mountain pass at 13,050 ft offering stunning views of glaciers and snow-capped peaks. Gateway to Lahaul-Spiti valley. Permits required (available online).

4.8 (5,210 reviews)
550 (Permit)
Iconic

Hadimba Temple

Unique wooden temple dedicated to Hidimba (from Mahabharata), surrounded by cedar forest. Famous for its intricate carvings and peaceful atmosphere.

Free Entry
Adventure

Solang Valley

Adventure hub offering paragliding, zorbing, skiing (Dec-Mar), and cable car rides. Stunning views of glaciers and snow-covered peaks.

Old Manali

Bohemian quarter with charming cafes, apple orchards, and hippie vibe. Great for leisurely walks, shopping for handicrafts, and enjoying live music.

Manali's Mountain Cuisine

Siddu

Traditional Himachali steamed bread made with wheat flour and stuffed with poppy seeds or walnuts. Served with ghee and lentil curry.

Best at: Traditional Himachali Dhabas

Trout Fish

Fresh river trout marinated in local spices and grilled to perfection. Specialty of Manali's riverside restaurants.

Best at: Johnson's Cafe

Mittha

Sweet rice dish prepared with dry fruits, saffron and sugar. Served during festivals and special occasions.

Best at: Local Home Kitchens

Tibetan Momos

Steamed dumplings with vegetable or meat fillings, served with spicy red chutney. Street food staple in Manali.

Best at: Tibetan Market Stalls

Aktori

Buckwheat pancake cooked during festivals, often served with honey or local cheese.

Best at: Traditional Homes

Babru

Himachali version of kachori - stuffed with black gram paste and deep fried. Served with tamarind chutney.

Best at: Mall Road Eateries

Best Time to Visit

Winter

-5°C - 10°C
Dec - Feb
Snow-covered landscapes perfect for skiing and snow activities. Heavy woolens required. Roads to Rohtang may be closed.

Spring

10°C - 20°C
Mar - Apr
Pleasant weather with blooming flowers. Ideal for trekking and sightseeing. Apple blossoms in orchards.

Summer

15°C - 25°C
May - Jun
Peak tourist season with perfect weather for all activities. Book accommodations in advance.

Monsoon

18°C - 28°C
Jul - Sep
Lush greenery but landslides may disrupt travel. Lower hotel rates and fewer crowds.
