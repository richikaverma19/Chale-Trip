Darjeeling, West Bengal

The Queen of the Himalayas - Tea Gardens, Toy Train & Majestic Kanchenjunga Views

15°C • Pleasant

Overview

Nestled in the Lesser Himalayas at an elevation of 2,042 meters (6,700 ft), Darjeeling is world-famous for its tea industry, the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), and stunning views of Kanchenjunga, the world's third-highest mountain. This charming hill station offers a perfect blend of colonial heritage, breathtaking landscapes, and vibrant local culture. From sunrise at Tiger Hill to exploring Buddhist monasteries and tea estates, Darjeeling promises unforgettable experiences for every traveler.

How to Reach Darjeeling

1 hr from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Bagdogra (IXB) - 70km away
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Kolkata, Guwahati
  • Taxi fare to Darjeeling: ₹2000-2500 (3-4 hrs)
  • Helicopter service available (15 min)
8-10 hrs from Kolkata

By Train

  • Nearest major station: New Jalpaiguri (NJP) - 62km
  • Toy Train from NJP to Darjeeling (7 hrs)
  • Recommended trains: Darjeeling Mail, Shatabdi Exp
  • Shared jeeps available from station
  • Scenic route through tea gardens
12-14 hrs from Kolkata

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH10 and NH55
  • Kolkata-Darjeeling via Siliguri (620km)
  • NBSTC buses (₹600-900 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹5000-7000 one way)
  • Stunning mountain roads with viewpoints

The Darjeeling Tea Experience

Darjeeling tea is renowned worldwide for its delicate aroma and distinctive muscatel flavor. Often called the "Champagne of Teas," it's grown in 87 tea gardens across the district. Experience tea plucking, processing, and tasting at estates like Happy Valley, Makaibari (world's first organic tea garden), or Glenburn (luxury tea estate stay). The best time to visit tea gardens is during the first flush (March-April) or second flush (May-June) seasons.
