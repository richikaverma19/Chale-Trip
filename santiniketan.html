About Santiniketan

Santiniketan, meaning "Abode of Peace", is a small town near Bolpur in West Bengal, established by Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore. It's home to Visva-Bharati University, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, where Tagore's vision of education in harmony with nature comes alive. The town is a vibrant center of art, culture, and learning, known for its open-air classrooms, beautiful gardens, and unique festivals. Santiniketan's artistic heritage includes the Bengal School of Art and the Kala Bhavana institute. The serene atmosphere, combined with its rich cultural programs, makes it a must-visit destination for those interested in India's artistic and intellectual heritage.

How to Reach Santiniketan

3-4 hrs from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International (CCU), Kolkata
  • Distance: 160km (3.5-4hr drive)
  • Taxi fare: ₹2500-3500 (AC)
  • Helicopter services available from Kolkata
2-3 hrs from station

By Train

  • Bolpur Shantiniketan (BHP) is the nearest station
  • Direct trains from Howrah (Kolkata): Shantiniketan Express, Rampurhat Express
  • Travel time: 2-3 hours from Howrah
  • Auto-rickshaws available from station to town (₹50-100)
4-5 hrs from Kolkata

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH114 and SH7
  • Private buses from Esplanade, Kolkata (₹200-400)
  • WBTC buses available (₹150-300)
  • Self-drive: Scenic route through rural Bengal

Major Festivals & Events

Poush Mela

December 23-25

Annual winter fair featuring Baul music, tribal dances, and handicraft stalls. Celebrates Bengali folk culture.

Varsha Mangal

July-August

Monsoon festival with Tagore songs, dance dramas, and cultural programs celebrating the rains.

Rabindra Jayanti

May 9

Tagore's birth anniversary celebrated with poetry readings, musical performances, and cultural events.

Nandan Mela

March

Art fair organized by Kala Bhavana students showcasing paintings, sculptures, and handicrafts.

Vasantotsav

Spring Season

Spring festival with students dancing in colorful attire, singing Tagore's seasonal songs.

Convocation

February 24

Visva-Bharati's annual convocation held under the trees, continuing Tagore's educational traditions.
