Where Sand Dunes Meet Snow-Capped Peaks

15°C - 25°C • Best Season: June-September

Discover Nubra Valley

Nubra Valley, often called the "Valley of Flowers" of Ladakh, is a breathtaking high-altitude desert valley located north of Leh. The trek takes you through a surreal landscape where snow-capped mountains contrast with sweeping sand dunes, and double-humped Bactrian camels roam amidst lush green villages. Crossing the famous Khardung La pass (5,359m), one of the highest motorable roads in the world, you'll descend into this hidden paradise with its ancient monasteries, apricot orchards, and the shimmering Shyok and Nubra rivers. The valley's unique culture blends Tibetan Buddhism with Central Asian influences, offering a truly distinctive Ladakhi experience.

Nubra Valley Highlights

Bactrian Camels

Ride the rare double-humped camels on Hunder sand dunes, remnants of the Silk Route trade.

Ancient Monasteries

Visit Diskit and Samstanling monasteries with their stunning murals and giant Buddha statues.

Khardung La Pass

Cross one of the world's highest motorable roads at 5,359m with breathtaking views.

Apricot Orchards

Walk through lush villages famous for their sweet apricots and walnut trees.

Confluence of Rivers

See where the Nubra and Shyok rivers meet, creating a stunning landscape.

Panamik Hot Springs

Relax in natural sulfur springs believed to have medicinal properties.

Nubra Valley Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Leh

0 km Acclimatization
  • Arrive at Leh Airport (3,500m)
  • Rest for acclimatization
  • Briefing and gear check
  • Overnight in Leh guesthouse

Day 2: Leh to Khardung La to Diskit

120 km drive 5-6 hours
  • Cross Khardung La (5,359m)
  • Visit Diskit Monastery
  • See 32m Maitreya Buddha statue
  • Overnight in Diskit

Day 3: Diskit to Hunder

10 km trek 4-5 hours
  • Camel safari on sand dunes
  • Explore apricot orchards
  • Stay in Hunder village
  • Spectacular night skies

Day 4: Hunder to Turtuk

85 km drive 3-4 hours
  • Last village before Pakistan
  • Meet Balti culture people
  • Explore ancient village
  • Homestay in Turtuk

Day 5: Turtuk to Panamik

70 km drive 3-4 hours
  • Relax in hot springs
  • Views of Siachen Glacier
  • River beach walks
  • Camp by the river

Day 6: Panamik to Leh

150 km drive 6-7 hours
  • Re-cross Khardung La
  • Farewell dinner in Leh
  • Last minute souvenir shopping
  • Overnight in Leh

Best Time to Visit

June

10°C - 20°C
Early Season
Pleasant temperatures. Snow melting on high passes. Fewer tourists. Some facilities may not be open.

July-August

15°C - 25°C
Peak Season
Warmest months with stable weather. All passes accessible. Apricots ripen in August. More crowded.

September

5°C - 18°C
Late Season
Cooler temperatures, clear skies. Harvest season in villages. Fewer tourists. Some facilities close.

May & October

-5°C - 15°C
Shoulder Season
Possible snow on passes. Few services available. Only for experienced travelers seeking solitude.
