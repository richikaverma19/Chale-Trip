McLeod Ganj, Himachal Pradesh

Little Lhasa of India - Tibetan Spiritual Capital

16°C • Pleasant

Overview

Nestled in the upper reaches of Dharamshala, McLeod Ganj is a vibrant hill station that serves as the home of the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. This spiritual and cultural hub blends Tibetan traditions with colonial charm, offering breathtaking mountain views, ancient monasteries, and a thriving arts scene. The town's narrow lanes are lined with prayer flags, Buddhist monasteries, and cozy cafes serving authentic Tibetan cuisine.

4.7/5 (2.1K reviews)

How to Reach McLeod Ganj

1.5 hr from airport

By Air

  • Gaggal Airport (DHM) - 20km from town
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Chandigarh
  • Major airlines: Air India, SpiceJet
  • Taxi fare to town: ₹1000-1500
  • Alternative: Pathankot airport (90km away)
10-12 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Nearest station: Pathankot (90km)
  • Direct trains from Delhi (Himachal Express)
  • Toy train from Pathankot to Kangra
  • Taxis available from Pathankot station
  • Scenic mountain railway journey
10-12 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH154 and NH503
  • Delhi-McLeod Ganj via Chandigarh, Una
  • HRTC Volvo buses to Dharamshala
  • Private taxi options (₹6000-8000 one way)
  • Beautiful mountain roads with scenic views

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

Tsuglagkhang Complex

The residence of the Dalai Lama and spiritual heart of Tibetan Buddhism in exile. Includes the Namgyal Monastery, Tibet Museum, and the Kalachakra Temple.

Namgyal Monastery

Triund Hill

Popular trekking destination offering panoramic views of the Dhauladhar range. Moderate 9km trek from McLeod Ganj, best done over 2 days.

Bhagsu Waterfall

Beautiful waterfall near Bhagsunag Temple, reached via a short trek. Refreshing spot to cool off in summer. Nearby cafes offer great mountain views.

McLeod Ganj's Cultural Cuisine

Thukpa

Hearty Tibetan noodle soup with vegetables or meat, flavored with garlic, ginger and local spices. Perfect for chilly mountain evenings.

Best at: Tibet Kitchen

Tibetan Momos

Steamed or fried dumplings with vegetable or meat fillings, served with fiery red chutney. Must-try street food in McLeod Ganj.

Best at: Kunga Restaurant

Thenthuk

Hand-pulled noodle soup with vegetables, a Tibetan comfort food. Hearty vegetarian meal after trekking.

Best at: Tibet Cafe

Shapta

Tibetan stir-fried beef dish with onions, peppers and spices. Served with tingmo (steamed bread).

Best at: Norling Restaurant

Tingmo

Steamed Tibetan bread, fluffy and slightly sweet. Perfect to soak up curry sauces or eat with butter tea.

Best at: Tibetan Momo Corner

Butter Tea

Traditional Tibetan tea made with yak butter and salt. Acquired taste but great for high altitude energy.

Best at: Local Tea Stalls

Best Time to Visit

Spring

8°C - 20°C
Mar - Apr
Pleasant weather with blooming rhododendrons. Ideal for sightseeing and light treks. Tibetan New Year celebrations in March.

Summer

12°C - 25°C
May - Jun
Perfect escape from plains' heat. Best for trekking and outdoor activities. Book accommodations in advance.

Monsoon

15°C - 22°C
Jul - Aug
Lush greenery but occasional landslides. Fewer tourists and lower prices. Carry rain gear.

Autumn

5°C - 18°C
Sep - Nov
Clear skies and comfortable temperatures. Best for photography and cultural festivals like Dussehra.
