Badrinath, Uttarakhand

Sacred Abode of Lord Vishnu - Where Divinity Meets the Himalayas

10°C • Cool

Overview

Nestled between the twin mountains of Nar and Narayan at an altitude of 3,133 meters (10,279 ft), Badrinath is one of Hinduism's most sacred pilgrimage sites. As one of the four Char Dhams, this temple town is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his Badri (berry) form. The ancient Badrinath Temple, established by Adi Shankaracharya in the 9th century, stands majestically along the banks of the Alaknanda River. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and hot springs, Badrinath offers a unique blend of spiritual significance and natural beauty that attracts devotees and travelers alike.

Sacred Sites & Attractions

Main Temple

Badrinath Temple

The sacred temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, featuring a black stone idol of Badrinarayan seated in meditation. The temple's striking architecture with a colorful facade stands against the dramatic Himalayan backdrop. Morning aarti is particularly divine.

4.9 (12K+ reviews)
Free Entry
Hot Springs

Tapt Kund

Natural hot sulfur spring (45°C) at the temple's base, considered medicinal. Pilgrims bathe here before entering the temple. The water is believed to have healing properties and is said to be the abode of Agni, the God of Fire.

4.7 (3,210 reviews)
Free Entry
Viewpoint

Brahma Kapal

Sacred flat platform on the banks of Alaknanda River where Hindus perform last rites. According to legend, Lord Brahma resides here. The evening Ganga aarti with Himalayan backdrop is a spiritually uplifting experience.

4.6 (1,890 reviews)
Free Entry
Trekking

Vasudhara Falls

Majestic 122m waterfall located 8km from Badrinath (3km trek from Mana village). According to legend, the waterfall turns away from unworthy visitors. The trek offers stunning valley views and passes through India's last village.

4.8 (1,250 reviews)
Free Entry
Village

Mana Village

India's last inhabited village before Tibet border, 3km from Badrinath. Explore ancient caves (Vyasa Gufa, Ganesh Gufa), woolen handicraft shops, and traditional stone houses. Try local buckwheat pancakes at village eateries.

4.5 (2,450 reviews)
Free Entry
Mythology

Bheem Pul

Natural rock bridge over Saraswati River near Mana village. According to Mahabharata, Bheem placed this massive rock to help Draupadi cross the river. The roaring Saraswati emerging from mountains is a spectacular sight.

4.4 (1,520 reviews)
Free Entry

Spiritual Activities in Badrinath

Morning Aarti at Badrinath Temple

Participate in the sacred morning rituals as priests perform elaborate puja to Lord Badrinarayan. The chanting of Vedic mantras amidst Himalayan peaks creates a divine atmosphere. Arrive by 4:30am for best experience.

Badrinath Temple

Trek to Valley of Flowers

UNESCO World Heritage Site located 46km from Badrinath. The moderate 5km trek (from Govindghat) leads through alpine meadows bursting with endemic flowers (July-Sept). Combine with visit to Hemkund Sahib for a full-day excursion.

Govindghat (base)

Explore Mana Village Culture

Visit India's last village before Tibet border. Interact with Bhotia tribe, see traditional wool weaving, explore Vyas Gufa (where Mahabharata was written), and taste local buckwheat dishes. Don't miss the woolen handicrafts.

Mana Village (3km away)

Holy Dip in Tapt Kund

Experience the therapeutic hot springs before temple visit. The sulfur-rich waters (45°C) are believed to cure skin ailments. Early morning baths are less crowded. Follow with darshan at Badrinath Temple for complete purification.

Below Badrinath Temple

Trek to Vasudhara Falls

Moderate 6km round-trip trek from Mana village to the spectacular 122m waterfall. Legend says the falls turn away from those with impure hearts. The trail offers stunning views of Neelkanth peak and Alaknanda valley.

Starts at Mana Village

Garhwali Cuisine Experience

Taste authentic Garhwali dishes like Kafuli (spinach curry), Chainsoo (black gram dal), Jhangora ki Kheer (millet pudding), and local rhododendron juice. Many eateries near temple serve traditional thalis with mountain ingredients.

Badrinath Market

Best Time to Visit

Opening Season

May-Jun
Spring
Temple opens late April/early May (date varies). Pleasant weather (5-18°C) with clear mountain views. Snow melting creates spectacular waterfalls. Ideal for avoiding monsoon crowds.

Peak Season

Jul-Aug
Monsoon
Warmer (10-20°C) but frequent rains can cause landslides. Lush green landscapes. Valley of Flowers at its best. Crowded during Char Dham Yatra. Carry rain gear.

Closing Season

Sep-Nov
Autumn
Temple closes around Diwali (Oct-Nov). Crisp weather (0-15°C) with clear skies. Fewer crowds as season ends. Chance of early snowfall in November.

Winter

Dec-Apr
Closed
Temple is closed due to heavy snowfall (-5 to -20°C). The deity moves to Joshimath's Narsimha Temple. Only extreme adventurers visit during winter.
