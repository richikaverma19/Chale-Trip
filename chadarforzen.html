Chadar Frozen River Trek

Walk on the Frozen Zanskar River in Extreme Winter Conditions

-15°C to -30°C • Season: January-February

The Chadar Trek Experience

The Chadar Trek is one of the most unique and challenging treks in the world, where you walk on the frozen Zanskar River in Ladakh during the peak of winter. This ancient trade route was traditionally used by locals when the summer paths became inaccessible. The trek takes you through spectacular frozen waterfalls, towering cliffs, and remote villages where life continues despite the extreme conditions. With temperatures dropping to -30°C at night, this is a true test of endurance and mental strength, offering an unparalleled adventure for those seeking to push their limits.

Why is Chadar So Challenging?

Extreme Cold

  • Daytime: -10°C to -15°C
  • Nighttime: -25°C to -30°C
  • Wind chill makes it feel colder
  • Risk of frostbite if unprepared
  • Batteries drain quickly

Changing Ice Conditions

  • Ice thickness varies daily
  • Some sections may be slushy
  • Need to walk through water sometimes
  • Ice can crack unexpectedly
  • Requires constant vigilance

Basic Facilities

  • Sleep in caves or tents on ice
  • No showers for 8-9 days
  • Limited toilet facilities
  • Simple vegetarian meals
  • No mobile network

Chadar Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Leh

0 km Acclimatization
  • Arrive at Leh Airport (3,500m)
  • Rest for acclimatization
  • Briefing and gear check
  • Overnight in Leh guesthouse

Day 2: Leh to Shingra Koma

65 km drive 4-5 hours
  • Drive to roadhead at Shingra Koma
  • Short walk to first campsite
  • Camp near frozen waterfall
  • First night on ice (-20°C)

Day 3: Shingra Koma to Tibb Cave

14 km trek 6-7 hours
  • First full day on frozen river
  • Dramatic canyon walls
  • Stay in large cave at Tibb
  • Bonfire inside the cave

Day 4: Tibb to Nerak Village

13 km trek 6-7 hours
  • Pass spectacular frozen waterfall
  • Reach remote Nerak village
  • Stay in local houses (warmer!)
  • Interact with Zanskari people

Days 5-8: Return Journey

60 km trek 4 days
  • Retrace steps back to start
  • Ice conditions may have changed
  • More time for photography
  • Different camping spots

Day 9: Return to Leh

65 km drive Celebration
  • Drive back to Leh
  • First proper shower in 9 days!
  • Celebration dinner
  • Certificate of completion

Unique Challenges of Chadar

Extreme Cold

Managing body temperature is crucial. Layering system must balance warmth without sweating (which can freeze).

Unpredictable Ice

Ice conditions change daily. Some sections may require walking through knee-deep freezing water.

Basic Accommodation

Sleeping in caves or tents on ice. No showers for 9 days. Toilet facilities are very basic.

Food & Hydration

Keeping water from freezing is challenging. Meals are simple vegetarian - no fresh produce available.
