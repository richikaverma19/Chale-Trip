Hatu Peak Trek

Conquer the highest point in Shimla at 3,400 meters for breathtaking Himalayan views

About Hatu Peak Trek

Hatu Peak is the highest point in the Shimla region, standing at an elevation of 3,400 meters (11,155 feet). This challenging yet rewarding trek takes you through dense forests of oak, pine, and rhododendron, culminating in panoramic views of the Greater Himalayas, including peaks like Shrikhand Mahadev, Kinner Kailash, and the Dhauladhar range.

Highlights: 360° Himalayan views, ancient Hatu Mata temple, dense forests, apple orchards, and a serene alpine environment.

Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Shimla to Narkanda (Drive) + Trek to Hatu Base Camp

  • Morning departure from Shimla (2.5-hour drive to Narkanda)
  • Start trek through lush forests and apple orchards
  • Gradual ascent towards Hatu Peak base camp
  • Reach camp by evening (5-6 hours trek)
  • Dinner and overnight stay in tents

Day 2: Summit Hatu Peak and Return

  • Early morning start for summit push (2-3 hours)
  • Explore the historic Hatu Mata temple at the summit
  • Spectacular views of the Himalayan ranges
  • Descend to Narkanda (4-5 hours)
  • Drive back to Shimla by evening

What to Expect

The Hatu Peak trek is rated as moderate to challenging due to its steep sections and high altitude. The trail offers diverse landscapes, from dense forests to open ridges. Key experiences include:

  • Dense forests of oak, pine, and rhododendron
  • Traditional Himachali villages along the route
  • Rich wildlife - spot Himalayan birds and occasionally even bears
  • The ancient Hatu Mata temple with wooden architecture
  • Unmatched views of snow-capped Himalayan peaks

Essential Information

Best Season: May to June and September to November

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging

Altitude: 3,400 meters (11,155 feet)

Trek Distance: 16 km round trip

Temperature: Day: 10-20°C, Night: 0-10°C (can drop below freezing in winter)
