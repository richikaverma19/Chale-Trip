The Sacred Chardham Yatra

The Chardham Yatra is one of Hinduism's most sacred pilgrimages, encompassing four holy sites nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand. This spiritual journey takes devotees to the sources of two sacred rivers (Yamunotri and Gangotri) and two of Lord Shiva and Vishnu's most revered shrines (Kedarnath and Badrinath).

Traditionally undertaken from west to east in the order: Yamunotri ‚Üí Gangotri ‚Üí Kedarnath ‚Üí Badrinath, this pilgrimage is believed to wash away sins and help attain moksha (liberation). The yatra typically opens in April/May and closes around Diwali (October/November) each year.

Yamunotri

Source of the Yamuna River

Yamunotri, at an elevation of 3,293 meters, is the westernmost shrine in the Chardham circuit. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Yamuna and the thermal springs at Janki Chatti are a major attraction.

Key Features:

  • Yamunotri Temple (built in 1839 by Tehri Naresh Sudarshan Shah)
  • Divya Shila - a rock pillar worshipped before entering temple
  • Surya Kund - boiling hot spring where rice is cooked as prasad
  • 6 km trek from Hanuman Chatti (or pony/doli available)
  • Best visited May-June and September-October

Gangotri

Source of the Ganges River

Located at 3,100 meters, Gangotri is where Goddess Ganga descended when Lord Shiva released her from his locks. The temple was built by Gorkha General Amar Singh Thapa in the 18th century.

Key Features:

  • Gangotri Temple (white marble structure)
  • Bhagirath Shila - rock where King Bhagirath meditated
  • Gaumukh Glacier - actual source 19km trek from Gangotri
  • Submerged Shivling - visible in winter when water recedes
  • Pandava Gufa - where Pandavas meditated during exile

Kedarnath

Abode of Lord Shiva

Perched at 3,583 meters amidst snow-capped peaks, Kedarnath is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas. The temple is believed to have been built by the Pandavas and revived by Adi Shankaracharya.

Key Features:

  • Kedarnath Temple (stone edifice of unknown antiquity)
  • Samadhi of Adi Shankaracharya behind the temple
  • Bhaironath Temple - protector of Kedarnath
  • 16 km trek from Gaurikund (or helicopter service available)
  • Extremely cold even in summer (carry woolens)

Badrinath

Abode of Lord Vishnu

Situated at 3,133 meters along the Alaknanda River, Badrinath is part of both Chardham and India's Char Dham. The temple was established by Adi Shankaracharya in the 9th century.

Key Features:

  • Badrinath Temple (brightly colored facade with gold gilt roof)
  • Tapt Kund - natural hot spring for ritual bath before darshan
  • Mana Village - last Indian village before Tibet border
  • Valley of Flowers & Hemkund Sahib nearby
  • Neelkanth Peak - stunning backdrop to the temple

Sample 12-Day Chardham Yatra Itinerary

Day 1-2: Arrival in Haridwar/Rishikesh

Arrival and acclimatization. Visit Har Ki Pauri, Mansa Devi Temple, and attend Ganga Aarti in Haridwar.

Day 3: Haridwar ‚Üí Barkot (220km/7-8hrs)

Travel to Barkot, the base for Yamunotri. Stop at Mussoorie for lunch with views of Doon Valley.

Day 4: Barkot ‚Üí Yamunotri ‚Üí Barkot (42km + 6km trek)

Early morning drive to Janki Chatti, then trek to Yamunotri. Return to Barkot in evening.

Day 5: Barkot ‚Üí Uttarkashi (160km/6-7hrs)

Drive to Uttarkashi via beautiful Himalayan valleys. Visit Kashi Vishwanath Temple in evening.

Day 6: Uttarkashi ‚Üí Gangotri ‚Üí Uttarkashi (100km each way)

Day trip to Gangotri. Attend evening aarti at the temple before returning to Uttarkashi.

Day 7: Uttarkashi ‚Üí Guptkashi (220km/8-9hrs)

Long drive through Tehri and along Mandakini River to reach Guptkashi, the base for Kedarnath.

Day 8: Guptkashi ‚Üí Kedarnath (30km + 16km trek)

Drive to Gaurikund, then trek to Kedarnath. Overnight at Kedarnath (or return same day by helicopter).

Day 9: Kedarnath ‚Üí Guptkashi

Morning darshan before descending back to Guptkashi. Rest and recuperate.

Day 10: Guptkashi ‚Üí Badrinath (170km/7-8hrs)

Drive through Joshimath to reach Badrinath. Evening visit to temple and Tapt Kund.

Day 11: Badrinath ‚Üí Rudraprayag (160km/6-7hrs)

Morning darshan and visit to Mana Village before driving back to Rudraprayag.

Day 12: Rudraprayag ‚Üí Haridwar (160km/5-6hrs)

Return journey with stops at Devprayag (confluence of Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers).
