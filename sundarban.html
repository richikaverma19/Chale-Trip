Sundarban National Park, West Bengal

Explore the Mystical Mangrove Kingdom of the Royal Bengal Tiger

28°C • Humid

Overview

The Sundarbans is a vast mangrove forest in the delta formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra and Meghna rivers in the Bay of Bengal. Spanning India and Bangladesh, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is famous for its Royal Bengal Tigers, saltwater crocodiles, and diverse marine life. The Indian side, Sundarban National Park, covers about 1,330 sq km of mangrove forests and water channels, home to over 100 tigers and numerous bird species. This unique ecosystem with its labyrinth of tidal waterways, mudflats and small islands is best explored by boat, offering an unforgettable wilderness experience.

Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary

Home to over 200 species of birds including kingfishers, herons, egrets, and the rare masked finfoot. Features a watchtower, crocodile pond, and the Sundarbans interpretation center. Best visited during winter migratory season.

Best Time to Visit

Winter (Nov-Feb)

15°C - 25°C
Peak Season
Pleasant weather with cool breezes. Best time for wildlife spotting and bird watching as migratory birds arrive. Clear skies and comfortable for boat safaris.

Spring (Mar-Apr)

25°C - 35°C
Shoulder Season
Temperature starts rising but still good for visits. Fewer tourists. Honey collection season begins - chance to see traditional methods.

Monsoon (May-Oct)

28°C - 35°C
Off Season
Hot and humid with heavy rains. Many areas inaccessible due to high tides. Some lodges closed. But lush greenery and dramatic skies for photography.
