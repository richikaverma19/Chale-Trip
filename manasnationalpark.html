Manas National Park, Assam

UNESCO World Heritage Site - Where Wilderness Meets the Himalayas

28°C • Humid

Overview

Manas National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and Project Tiger Reserve, is one of India's most biodiverse protected areas. Located in Assam at the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas and bordering Bhutan, this park spans 950 sq km of grasslands, tropical forests, and riverine ecosystems. Famous for its population of wild water buffalo, rare golden langurs, and the elusive Bengal tiger, Manas offers unparalleled wildlife viewing opportunities. The park is named after the Manas River and is home to over 55 mammal species, 450 bird species, and 50 reptile species, making it a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife photographers.

One-Horned Rhinos

Manas is home to the endangered Indian one-horned rhinoceros, successfully reintroduced after poaching threats. Best spotted during elephant safaris in the Mathanguri area or jeep safaris in Bhuyanpara range.

Best Time to Visit

Winter (Nov-Feb)

10°C - 25°C
Peak Season
Pleasant weather with minimal rain. Best for wildlife sightings as animals gather near water sources. Excellent birdwatching with migratory species.

Spring (Mar-Apr)

18°C - 32°C
Pre-Monsoon
Warming temperatures with lush green landscapes. Good for seeing newborn animals. Morning safaris are most comfortable.

Monsoon (May-Oct)

25°C - 35°C
Park Closed
Heavy rainfall makes most areas inaccessible. Park typically closes from May to October for monsoon season.
