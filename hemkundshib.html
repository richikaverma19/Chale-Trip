Hemkund Sahib, Uttarakhand

The Sacred High-Altitude Sikh Shrine - Surrounded by Seven Snow-Capped Peaks

5°C • Chilly

Overview

Hemkund Sahib, at an altitude of 4,632 meters (15,197 feet), is one of the most sacred Sikh shrines in the world. This high-altitude gurudwara is situated beside a glacial lake surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks. According to Sikh tradition, Guru Gobind Singh meditated here in his previous birth. The journey to Hemkund Sahib is as spiritually significant as the destination itself, passing through breathtaking Himalayan landscapes.

Hemkund Lake

The pristine glacial lake beside the gurudwara, surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks. Pilgrims take a holy dip in the icy waters before entering the gurudwara. The lake remains frozen for most of the year except during pilgrimage season.

Hemkund Sahib's Local Cuisine

Gurudwara Langar

Simple vegetarian meals served free to all visitors at Hemkund Sahib and enroute gurudwaras. Typically includes dal, roti, rice, sabzi and kheer. Prepared with devotion by volunteers.

Available at: All Gurudwaras enroute

Aloo Ke Gutke

Traditional Garhwali dish of boiled potatoes tempered with local spices like jakhiya and bhangjeera. Served with puris or rotis, provides energy for the trek.

Best at: Ghagaria village eateries

Buransh Tea

Herbal tea made from rhododendron flowers, a local specialty that helps with altitude acclimatization. Served hot with ginger and lemon.

Best at: Trek route tea stalls

Local Energy Bars

Homemade energy bars with nuts, seeds, jaggery and local grains. Perfect trekking snack providing instant energy at high altitudes.

Best at: Govindghat market

Bhang Ki Chutney

Spicy chutney made from hemp seeds, a local delicacy in Garhwal region. Served with meals or as accompaniment, said to help with altitude.

Best at: Local dhabas enroute

Karah Prasad

Sacred sweet offering made with wheat flour, sugar and ghee. Distributed at all Sikh gurudwaras after prayers, considered spiritually blessed food.

Available at: Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

Best Time to Visit

Early Season

5°C - 15°C
June - July
Gurudwara opens in June after snow clearance. Cool weather with some snow patches remaining. Fewer crowds but Valley of Flowers not in full bloom.

Peak Season

10°C - 20°C
Aug - Sep
Ideal time with pleasant weather. Valley of Flowers in full bloom. More facilities available but also more pilgrims.

Late Season

0°C - 10°C
Oct
Gurudwara closes by Diwali (Oct-Nov). Chilly weather with chance of early snowfall. Fewer visitors, serene atmosphere.
