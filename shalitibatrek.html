Shali Tibba Trek

Conquer Shimla's highest peak at 2,872 meters for breathtaking panoramic views of the Himalayas

About Shali Tibba Trek

Shali Tibba is the highest peak in the Shimla district, standing tall at 2,872 meters (9,423 feet) above sea level. This moderate trek offers one of the most spectacular 360-degree views of the Himalayan ranges, including peaks like Chanshal, Kinnaur Kailash, and even glimpses of the Dhauladhar range on clear days.

Highlights: Panoramic mountain views, ancient Shali Mata temple, dense forests, remote villages, and stunning sunrise/sunset points.

Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Shimla to Khatnol (Drive) + Trek to Shali Tibba Base

  • Morning departure from Shimla (2-hour drive to Khatnol)
  • Start trek through beautiful oak and rhododendron forests
  • Pass through traditional Himachali villages
  • Reach base camp by evening (5-6 hours trek)
  • Dinner and overnight stay in tents

Day 2: Summit Shali Tibba and Return

  • Early morning start for summit attempt (2-3 hours)
  • Explore the ancient Shali Mata temple at the top
  • 360-degree views of Himalayan peaks
  • Descend to Khatnol (4-5 hours)
  • Drive back to Shimla by evening

What to Expect

The Shali Tibba trek is rated as moderate difficulty. The trail involves steady ascents through mixed forests and open meadows. The final climb to the summit is steep but manageable for anyone with reasonable fitness. You'll experience:

  • Diverse landscapes from dense forests to alpine meadows
  • Traditional mountain villages with slate-roofed houses
  • Rich biodiversity - spot monal pheasants and other Himalayan birds
  • The ancient Shali Mata temple with its unique architecture
  • Unparalleled views of multiple Himalayan ranges

Essential Information

Best Season: April to June and September to November

Difficulty: Moderate (requires basic fitness)

Altitude: 2,872 meters (9,423 feet)

Trek Distance: 14 km round trip

Temperature: Day: 15-25°C, Night: 5-10°C (can drop below 0°C in winter)
