Kashmir Great Lakes Trek

Discover the most beautiful alpine lakes in the Himalayas on this 7-day trek through paradise

About Kashmir Great Lakes Trek

The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful treks in India. This 7-day journey takes you through some of the most stunning alpine lakes in the Himalayas, each more beautiful than the last. The trek passes through lush green meadows, pine forests, and high mountain passes with breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks.

This trek is often described as "heaven on earth" and offers the perfect combination of adventure and natural beauty, making it a must-do for every trekking enthusiast.

Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Srinagar to Sonamarg (Drive) + Trek to Nichnai

  • Drive from Srinagar to Sonamarg (3 hours)
  • Start trek through pine forests and meadows
  • Gradual ascent to Nichnai campsite
  • Trekking time: 6-7 hours
  • Overnight camping at 11,500 ft

Day 2: Nichnai to Vishansar Lake via Nichnai Pass

  • Steep climb to Nichnai Pass (13,500 ft)
  • Stunning views of the surrounding peaks
  • Descend to Vishansar Lake campsite
  • Trekking time: 7-8 hours
  • Camp by the pristine Vishansar Lake

Day 3: Vishansar Lake to Gadsar via Gadsar Pass

  • Visit Krishansar Lake en route
  • Challenging climb to Gadsar Pass (13,750 ft)
  • Descend to Gadsar Lake and campsite
  • Trekking time: 6-7 hours
  • Camp near the beautiful Gadsar Lake

Day 4: Gadsar to Satsar Lakes

  • Relatively easier day of trekking
  • Pass through beautiful meadows and streams
  • Reach the cluster of Satsar Lakes
  • Trekking time: 5-6 hours
  • Camp near the Satsar Lakes

Day 5: Satsar to Gangabal Twin Lakes

  • Cross the Zajibal Pass (13,000 ft)
  • First views of the stunning Gangabal Lakes
  • Descend to Nundkol and Gangabal Lakes
  • Trekking time: 6-7 hours
  • Camp with spectacular views of Harmukh peak

Day 6: Gangabal Lakes to Naranag (Trek) + Drive to Srinagar

  • Gradual descent through pine forests
  • Visit ancient Naranag temple complex
  • Drive back to Srinagar (3 hours)
  • Trekking time: 4-5 hours
  • Overnight in Srinagar (hotel stay)

What to Expect

The Kashmir Great Lakes trek is rated as moderate to difficult and offers:

  • Seven stunning high-altitude alpine lakes
  • Diverse landscapes from meadows to rocky passes
  • Views of snow-capped peaks including Harmukh
  • Rich biodiversity - spot Himalayan marmots and trout
  • Authentic Kashmiri cultural experiences
  • Challenging but rewarding high mountain passes

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Kashmir Great Lakes trek

What is the best time to do this trek?

The best time for the Kashmir Great Lakes trek is from July to September when the weather is pleasant and the lakes are at their most beautiful. The snow has melted by July, and the meadows are covered with wildflowers. September offers clearer skies but can be colder at night.

How difficult is the Kashmir Great Lakes trek?

The trek is rated as moderate to difficult. While the daily walking distances aren't extremely long (5-8 hours), the high altitude passes (especially Gadsar Pass at 13,750 ft) make it challenging. You should have good cardiovascular fitness and preferably some prior trekking experience.

What kind of accommodation is provided?

During the trek, you'll stay in high-quality tents (twin sharing) with sleeping bags and sleeping mats provided. In Srinagar at the start and end of the trek, you'll stay in a comfortable hotel. All camping equipment is provided by us - you just need to bring your personal items.

What about food and water during the trek?

We provide nutritious vegetarian meals throughout the trek, prepared by our experienced cooks. Breakfast typically includes porridge, eggs, toast, and tea/coffee. Lunch is packed for the trail, and dinner is a hot meal with soup, rice, dal, vegetables, and dessert. Water is collected from natural streams and boiled/filtered for safety.

What should I pack for this trek?

Essential items include: trekking shoes, warm layers (thermals, fleece, down jacket), rain gear, sun protection (hat, sunglasses, sunscreen), water bottles, headlamp, personal medications, and toiletries. We provide a detailed packing list upon booking. Porters/mules carry the main luggage - you only need to carry a daypack with essentials.
