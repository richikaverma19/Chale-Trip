Kedarnath, Uttarakhand

Abode of Lord Shiva - Where Spirituality Meets the Himalayas

5°C • Chilly

Overview

Perched at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 ft) near the Mandakini river in the Garhwal Himalayas, Kedarnath is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites for Hindus. It is the most remote of the four Chota Char Dham pilgrimage sites and home to the ancient Kedarnath Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple, believed to be over 1,200 years old, stands against the backdrop of the majestic Kedarnath peak. The journey to Kedarnath is as spiritual as the destination itself, with breathtaking Himalayan views and a challenging trek that tests devotees' resolve.

Sacred Sites & Attractions

Main Temple

Kedarnath Temple

The ancient stone temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, built by Adi Shankaracharya in 8th century. The lingam inside is a natural rock formation. The temple's survival during the 2013 floods is considered miraculous by devotees.

5.0 (10K+ reviews)
Free Entry
Nature

Vasuki Tal

High-altitude glacial lake (4,135m) located 8km from Kedarnath. The challenging trek offers stunning views of Chaukhamba peaks. Considered sacred, the lake is named after the serpent king Vasuki who served as rope during Samudra Manthan.

4.8 (1,210 reviews)
Free Entry
Heritage

Bhairavnath Temple

Dedicated to Bhairav, the fierce manifestation of Shiva who protects Kedarnath in winter when the main temple is closed. Located 500m from main temple with panoramic valley views. Evening aarti is particularly powerful here.

4.7 (890 reviews)
Free Entry
Adventure

Chorabari Tal (Gandhi Sarovar)

Scenic lake located 3km from Kedarnath at 3,900m altitude. Some of Mahatma Gandhi's ashes were immersed here. The trek passes through beautiful meadows with views of surrounding peaks. Best visited in morning hours.

4.6 (750 reviews)
Free Entry
Pilgrimage

Gaurikund

The starting point of the Kedarnath trek with hot water springs where Parvati meditated to win Shiva. Features ancient Gauri Temple and bathing tanks. Pilgrims traditionally take a holy dip here before beginning the trek.

4.5 (1,450 reviews)
Free Entry
Museum

Samadhi of Adi Shankaracharya

Located behind Kedarnath temple, this is believed to be the final resting place of the 8th-century philosopher who revived Hinduism. The site offers peaceful meditation space with views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks.

4.7 (1,020 reviews)
Free Entry

Spiritual Activities in Kedarnath

Morning Aarti at Kedarnath

Participate in the sacred morning rituals at Kedarnath Temple as priests perform elaborate puja to Lord Shiva. The chanting of Vedic mantras amidst Himalayan peaks creates a divine atmosphere. Arrive by 4:30am for best experience.

Kedarnath Temple

Trek to Vasuki Tal

Challenging 16km round-trip trek to the high-altitude glacial lake. The trail offers spectacular views of Himalayan peaks and passes through rugged terrain. Best started early morning with a guide. Altitude makes this trek demanding.

Starts behind Kedarnath Temple

Evening Ganga Aarti at Sonprayag

Witness the beautiful evening aarti ceremony at the confluence of Mandakini and Songanga rivers. Priests perform rituals with fire lamps as devotees float diyas in the river. The spiritual atmosphere is enhanced by Himalayan backdrop.

Sonprayag (en route)

Meditation at Bhairavnath Temple

Experience deep meditation in the serene environment of Bhairavnath Temple with panoramic Himalayan views. The early morning hours are particularly powerful for spiritual practices. Local guides can teach basic Himalayan meditation techniques.

Bhairavnath Temple

Chorabari Tal (Gandhi Sarovar) Trek

Moderate 6km round-trip trek to the scenic glacial lake where some of Gandhi's ashes were immersed. The trail offers beautiful views of Kedarnath peak and surrounding glaciers. Best done between 8am-2pm for clear views.

North of Kedarnath Temple

Explore Local Garhwali Culture

Interact with the resilient mountain communities who maintain the pilgrimage route. Visit local tea stalls to hear stories of the region's spiritual significance and learn about traditional Garhwali customs and cuisine.

Kedarnath Village

Best Time to Visit

Opening Season

May-Jun
Spring
Temple opens late April/early May (date varies). Pleasant weather (5-15°C) with clear views. Snow melting creates gushing waterfalls. Ideal for those wanting to avoid monsoon.

Peak Season

Jun-Sep
Summer
Warmer temperatures (10-18°C) but monsoon rains (July-Aug) can cause landslides. Crowded during Char Dham Yatra season. Best for those who don't mind rain and want full pilgrimage experience.

Closing Season

Oct-Nov
Autumn
Temple closes around Diwali (Oct-Nov). Crisp weather (0-10°C) with clear mountain views. Fewer crowds as season ends. Chance of early snowfall in November.

Winter

Dec-Apr
Closed
Temple is closed due to heavy snowfall (-5 to -15°C). The deity's symbolic presence moves to Ukhimath. Only extreme adventurers visit during winter via challenging routes.
