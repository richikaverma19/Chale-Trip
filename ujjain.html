Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh

The Sacred City of Mahakal - One of India's Seven Moksha Puris

28°C • Pleasant

Overview

Ujjain, one of the seven sacred cities (Sapta Puri) of Hinduism, is an ancient city on the eastern bank of the Shipra River. Known as the city of Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, Ujjain has been a major center of religion, culture, and learning since the 6th century BCE. The city's spiritual significance is matched by its historical importance as the capital of the Avanti Kingdom and a prominent center of astronomy where great scholars like Brahmagupta and Bhaskaracharya made significant contributions.

Ujjain's Local Cuisine

Poha Jalebi

Classic Ujjain breakfast combo - flattened rice cooked with spices, served with crispy sweet jalebis. A perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors.

Best at: Shree Ji Pavitra Bhojnalaya

Dal Bafla

MP's version of dal bati - wheat dough balls baked and dipped in ghee, served with spicy dal and chutney. Hearty traditional meal.

Best at: Bade Ganeshji Ka Dhaba

Mawa Bati

Rich sweet made from khoya (reduced milk), shaped like bafla and soaked in sugar syrup. Ujjain's specialty dessert.

Best at: Joshi Doodh Bhandar

Kachori Sabzi

Fluffy fried kachoris served with spicy potato curry and sweet tamarind chutney. Popular street food snack.

Best at: Mahakal Street Vendors

Mahakal Laddu

Special prasad offered at Mahakaleshwar Temple, made from besan, sugar and dry fruits. Believed to be blessed by Lord Shiva.

Available at: Temple Prasad Counter

Bhutte Ka Kees

Grated corn cooked with milk and spices, a monsoon specialty. Creamy texture with subtle sweetness from fresh corn.

Best at: Agarwal Restaurant
