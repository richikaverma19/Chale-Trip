Trek Overview

Experience one of Northeast India's most beautiful treks through the enchanting Dzukou Valley

The Valley of Flowers of the East

Dzukou Valley, located at the border of Nagaland and Manipur, is famous for its seasonal flowers and the rare Dzukou lily. The valley transforms into a colorful carpet of blooms during monsoon, while winter brings a magical frost-covered landscape.

This moderate trek takes you through lush green hills, bamboo forests, and crystal-clear streams. The valley remains relatively untouched by commercialization, offering trekkers a pristine natural experience.

Trek Highlights

  • Breathtaking views of rolling hills covered in flowers
  • Stay in cozy bamboo huts at the valley base
  • Visit to local Naga villages and cultural immersion
  • Chance to spot rare flora like the Dzukou lily
  • Campfire under starry skies

Trek Essentials

What to bring and know before you go on the Dzukou Valley trek

What to Pack

  • Sturdy trekking shoes
  • Backpack (40-50 liters)
  • Warm layers (temperatures drop at night)
  • Raincoat/poncho
  • Water bottles (2 liters capacity)
  • Headlamp/flashlight
  • Personal medications

Food & Accommodation

  • All meals included during trek
  • Simple vegetarian/Naga cuisine
  • Bamboo huts at base camp
  • Village homestay on Day 3
  • Carry energy bars/snacks if desired
  • Purified drinking water provided

Important Notes

  • Moderate fitness level required
  • Acclimatize to altitude properly
  • No plastic policy in valley
  • Respect local customs
  • October-May best for flowers
  • Winter treks possible (colder)
